EXPLANATION OF THE PROJECT

Why Bikes?

Bycicles have been around for ages. For people, it can be a means of transportation, with the major cities already pushing for an integrated system with the city. This means that the number of users is growing exponentially, stemed mainly by millenials and pollution-conscious people. On other instances, it is a cheaper alternative of commuting. As a biker (and commuter) myself, I have two things in mind. First, what is the cost of using the bikes. Second, what is the availability and easyness of use.

Motivation

This motivates me to use analytics and data science to understand the Capital Bikeshare system and augment my experience. I want to know how congestioned are the routes I plan to take, how reliable is the system (will I always find a bike?) How can I minimize my commuting expense by knowing before hand if I will pass the 30 minute limit.

How is it helping?

Users (both residents and tourists) want to minimize the cost of riding the red bikes. The cheaper it is for the user, the better. That is of course, without sacrificing reliability or easyness. From a company perspective, the bottom line is not to maximize profit (they are a non-profit) but rather to provide a reliable and affordable transportation system for residents of DC.

Next Steps

With more than 430 stations, 3700+ bikes, 5.7k rides daily and over 2.1 million rides annually, there is a lot of data to wrangle. We will start by exploring the stations and then we will analize a subset of trips. This will be the stepping stone to running the analysis over the whole historic data.
